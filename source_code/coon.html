What Do Maine Coon Cats Eat?


Maine Coon cats eat a combination of meat like chicken and fish, dry cat food, and wet cat food.

Main Coons are large and energetic, so they need plenty of protein to fuel their play. While they don’t need a specialized diet per se, they need to consume raw or cooked meat.

While these cats can get all of their dietary needs through dry and wet cat food, some owners opt for a raw food diet combined with supplements. Stick with high-protein, low-fat meat like poultry, fish, and rabbit. The best raw food diet for Maine Coon cats contains 85% muscle meat, organs, and ground bones. If you choose this diet for your cat, consider adding vitamins and supplements to keep your cat healthy.

Veterinary studies confirmed that omega-3 fatty acid supplements help treat inflammatory bowel disease in Maine Coons.

Most people keep Maine Coon cats as pets and feed them a diet like regular domestic cats. This is perfectly acceptable as long as their daily protein intake is 35% to 50%. When picking out cat food, ensure the first ingredient is protein, like fish or chicken.

Choose high-quality cat foods that contain fewer carbs and moderate amounts of fat. Maine Coons are predisposed to adult diabetes and obesity, so ingredients like grains and corn should be limited.

You can give Maine Coons dry cat food daily and wet cat food two to three times a week as their primary diet. Also, veterinary studies confirmed that omega-3 fatty acid supplements help treat inflammatory bowel disease in Maine Coons.


What Can Maine Coons Not Eat?

Like all domestic cats, Maine Coons must steer clear of certain types of food. Eating the wrong foods can cause digestion problems, high temperatures, abdominal pain, and increased thirst. It’s rare but possible for Maine Coons to suffer from seizures and even death when given the wrong food in high quantities.

Do not feed your Maine Coon:

  • Chocolate

  • Dairy products like milk

  • Alcohol

  • Candy and other sugary products

  • Large amounts of tuna fish

  • Grapes and raisins

  • Onions and garlic

  • Mushrooms

  • Caffeinated drinks

  • Dog food

  • Human medicines
