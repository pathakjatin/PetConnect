Dalmatian

Diet and Nutrition
Dalmatians have specific dietary needs. This is due, in part, to their propensity for urinary issues, notably urate stones. To help prevent these problems, it's crucial to feed them a low-purine diet. Look for dog food that's specially designed for Dalmatians or breeds prone to urinary issues. These diets often include reduced levels of purines, which can help minimize the formation of these stones.
Excersize and Activity needs
Dalmatians are an extremely energetic breed that requires regular exercise to be happy, healthy, and content (Coile, 271). In general, experts agree that this particular breed should receive 30 to 60 minutes of vigorous exercise daily. This can include long walks, jogging, or one-on-one play.
0 to 3 weeks
Puppies should consume their mother’s milk for the first 3 weeks of their life. For the next two weeks after that, they can be offered a bit of puppy dog food, but their mom still needs to be feeding puppies most of their nutrition. From the age of 6 weeks onwards, they can start to eat part of their meals on their own and get part of their nutrients from their mother's milk. By 8 weeks, they can get most of their nutrition by themselves from puppy dog food. It’s important for a puppy’s development to get its mother’s milk in this initial stage. It builds their immune system and helps them get the right nutrients which set the stage for long-term health.
